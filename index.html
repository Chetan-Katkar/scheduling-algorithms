Algorithms

An algorithm is a step-by-step procedure designed to perform a specific task or solve a problem efficiently.
It follows a well-defined sequence of operations, ensuring consistent and repeatable results.Algorithms are the backbone of
computing, playing a crucial role in data processing, artificial intelligence, cryptography, and operating systems. In scheduling,
algorithms manage how tasks are executed by the CPU, optimizing resource utilization and improving system performance. Various
scheduling techniques like FCFS, Round Robin,Priority Scheduling, and SRTN determine the order of execution based on predefined rules.
Understanding these algorithms helps in designing efficient systems, reducing waiting time, and enhancing overall computing speed.

Simulations

A simulation is a process of mimicking the behavior of a real-world system to analyze and understand its performance under different conditions.
In computing, simulations are widely used to test algorithms, model system behavior, and optimize decision-making without affecting actualoperations.
In scheduling, simulations help visualize how different algorithms manage CPU processes, allocate resources, and handle multitasking in an operating system.
By running a simulation, you can observe key performance metrics like waiting time, turnaround time, and response time, allowing for a deeper
understanding of scheduling efficiency.This website offers an interactive Simulation Section where you can input custom values, run scheduling algorithms,
and observe real-time results. This hands-on approach helps bridge the gap between theoretical concepts and practical implementation.
